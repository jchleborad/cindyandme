6’ From the Edge

(Horseshoe Bend, March 30th)

As we came up to the parking lot all I could think was, “I know I read they have restrooms!” It has been a long haul from Flagstaff, and we were desperate. I think it was after we returned to the car we realized we were at Horseshoe Bend, one of the must-do’s in Page, Arizona. It was also one of the first moments we realized not everything is how it seems.

You see, in order to get to the cliffs to see the river you must hike a little over half a mile. The trail is all downhill, and sandy. I mean the finest sand you have seen. Here we were in the middle of the high desert walking as though we were on a beach. It is slow going.

John has a theory about canyons, if he stays six feet from the edge he is safe. Of course this can really hamper your view if the sight is almost straight down. So eventually he got up enough nerve to stand about 5’6” from the edge. Just enough for his eyes to see below, and it was worth it!

This is such an amazing place! John upon seeing the view

As I stood 2’-3’ feet from the edge, all I could think is that water will always choose the path of least resistance. How could bending like a horseshoe ever be easy path? This make no logical sense to me. But then you see it and you understand that the sense is not in the path of the river, but in what is being taught to us. The beauty is in the journey, not in the shortest route.
