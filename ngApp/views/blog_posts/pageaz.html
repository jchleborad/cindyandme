6’ From the Edge

(Horseshoe Bend, March 30th)

As we came up to the parking lot all I could think was, “I know I read they have restrooms!” It has been a long haul from Flagstaff, and we were desperate. I think it was after we returned to the car we realized we were at Horseshoe Bend, one of the must-do’s in Page, Arizona. It was also one of the first moments we realized not everything is how it seems.

You see, in order to get to the cliffs to see the river you must hike a little over half a mile. The trail is all downhill, and sandy. I mean the finest sand you have seen. Here we were in the middle of the high desert walking as though we were on a beach. It is slow going.

John has a theory about canyons, if he stays six feet from the edge he is safe. Of course this can really hamper your view if the sight is almost straight down. So eventually he got up enough nerve to stand about 5’6” from the edge. Just enough for his eyes to see below, and it was worth it!

This is such an amazing place! John upon seeing the view

As I stood 2’-3’ feet from the edge, all I could think is that water will always choose the path of least resistance. How could bending like a horseshoe ever be easy path? This make no logical sense to me. But then you see it and you understand that the sense is not in the path of the river, but in what is being taught to us. The beauty is in the journey, not in the shortest route.

I am excited for the rest of our weekend travels

Antelope Canyon

Morning Butterflies

This morning I woke up with butterflies in my stomach because by lunch time I will be crossing an item off my bucket list. I remember this feeling when I was a little girl specifically waking up at Laura’s house heading to Universal Studios to celebrate her birthday, dance competitions in San Gabriel, and leaving for vacation. As an adult I still get this same feeling on days when I will be blessed with a once in a life time experience such as our wedding day, starting a sought-after job, leaving to share an experience with our children, and moving the children into their first home away from home.

As part of my birthday John arranged for us to take a tour of Antelope Canyon! The canyon is one of God’s miracle creations. The scenes are touted as breath-taking. In fact, I may be able to cross two things off my bucket list...taking that “one” picture.

Evening Impressions

Antelope Canyon was more miraculous than I could have imagined! I have seen pictures and more pictures of this hidden slot canyon. I thought I would be impressed, but instead it was a feeling of total awe that can only be brought on by unique beauty that blesses you beyond what you feel you deserve.

What did I expect? Well, I expected a few spots to take a few pictures that are over printed and for sale in tourist traps throughout Arizona. On the contrary the experience was one that yielded over 100 captures, and a feeling that 100’s more were unseen. With every twist and turn I found my self trying to look up, look down and circle in a 360 in effort not to miss anything, and I know going back would be a fresh experience.

Maybe someday…

In Pursuit

The Perfect (Kokopelli) Pot

If Highway 89A is considered one of the paths least traveled in Arizona, they clearly were not on our path today. The journey began about 3:30 yesterday and ended in a moment of grit determination.

Yesterday at roadside stop John decided that he would like a Navajo Pot to put in his new office. He has been looking decor, and I thought it was a great idea. This was so much better than anything that could be picked up at Hobby Lobby. This would have some meaning for him. Who knows, maybe seeing the pot daily will inspire the planning of our next adventure. Needless to say, I was all for it, that is until I realized it had to be the perfect pot.

This morning we hopped on the highway headed back home with the plan to stop at some roadside vendors hoping to find the perfect pot. Let’s just say if the government thought we were interesting enough to track even they would have been confused.

  • 1st stop: Closed
  • 2nd non-stop at an outlook and we buzzed right by
  • 3rd stop decided not to stop because it was 14 miles off the highway
  • 4th stop roadside stand with 3 over priced choices
  • 5th stop back up the hill to the lookout
  • 6th stop roadside stand outside of Gap, Arizona
  • 7th stop trading post 100 yards down the road from stop 6
  • 8th stop back to stop 6 to purchase the pot
  • 9th stop back to the car because the Square for the sale wasn’t working
  • 10th stop back to buy the pot with money scraped together
  • 11-23rd stops the number of times we didn’t stop, but John noted places he could have stopped wondering if they might have a pot he liked better.
